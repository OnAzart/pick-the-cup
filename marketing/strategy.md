# Strategy

One idea drives everything: **the bracket card is the content, the app is a footnote.** Nobody shares an app link. People share their own opinion with a picture, and the picture happens to have our name on it.

## Why now

Quarterfinals start in a few days. This is the peak window — everyone has an opinion, most results are still open. After the semis the bracket is basically decided and the whole mechanic dies. Ship posts now, not next week.

## The loop

1. Post a bracket card with one deliberately spicy pick.
2. People argue in the comments. Arguments = reach.
3. Reply to arguers with "make yours then" + link. The app auto-generates their card and challenge link.
4. Their card carries the same footer. Repeat.

## Channel roles

- **Twitter/X** — main arena. Quote-tweet mechanics fit brackets perfectly ("quote me with yours"). Post daily around match kickoffs.
- **Reddit** — biggest single-post upside, hostile to promo. Image post + hot take title, link only in comments when asked. One shot per sub, don't spam.
- **Threads** — football Twitter refugees, decent organic reach for small accounts. Same content as X, slightly warmer tone.
- **Instagram** — stories with the card + poll sticker ("agree?"). Feed post only for milestone moments (bracket dead / called the final).
- **WhatsApp/Telegram** — not posts. Send your card to group chats directly. This is where the challenge link actually converts.

## Timing beats

- **Before each QF**: "my bracket says X, kickoff in 3 hours" post.
- **Right after a result**: "called it" or "there goes my bracket" within 15 minutes of full-time. Fastest post wins the reaction traffic.
- **Before semis/final**: leaderboard bragging ("#12 of 2,400").

## Rules

- One spicy pick per post. "France out in the semi" starts fights; "Argentina wins" is wallpaper.
- Reply to every quote/comment with a comparison to your own card.
- Never post the same text twice; the card image can repeat, words can't.
- No hashtag walls. #PickTheCup at most, it's already on the card.
